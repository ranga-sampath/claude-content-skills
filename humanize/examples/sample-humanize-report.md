---

**Em Dashes**
- Before: `There is a moment every builder hits — the moment when the off-the-shelf tool stops being enough.`
- After: `There's a moment every builder hits. The moment when the off-the-shelf tool stops being enough.`
- Why: Em dash replaced with a period; sentence split into two. Contraction added to the opener while here.

- Before: `the same context, the same analysis framework, the same output format — all applied automatically.`
- After: `the same context, the same analysis framework, the same output format, all applied without thinking.`
- Why: Em dash replaced with a comma. "Automatically" replaced with "without thinking" — more active, less mechanical.

---

**Importance-Signaling Adverbs**
- Before: `Importantly, this was not a failure of the model.`
- After: `Not a failure of the model.`
- Why: "Importantly" removed; sentence restructured to say the thing directly.

- Before: `Notably, the improvement was not just in speed. It was in quality.`
- After: `The improvement wasn't just speed. It was consistency.`
- Why: "Notably" removed; contraction added; "in speed" / "in quality" tightened to "speed" / "consistency".

---

**Contractions in Declarations**
- Before: `It was not that Claude was performing poorly.`
- After: `It wasn't that Claude was performing poorly.`
- Why: Full form replaced with contraction in a casual, personal sentence.

- Before: `Every senior engineer who has reviewed an AI output...`
- After: `Every senior engineer who's reviewed an AI output...`
- Why: Contraction added.

---

**Restore First-Person Voice**
- Before: `It was that I was asking it to do the same technical analysis repeatedly...`
- After: `I was asking it to do the same technical analysis repeatedly...`
- Why: "It was that" framing removed; sentence opens with the subject doing the thing.

---

**Corporate Prepositions and Formal Phrasing**

- Why: "Prior to" replaced with "Before I"; more natural.
